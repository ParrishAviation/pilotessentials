# The Four Forces of Flight

The four forces acting on an aircraft in flight are:

## 1. **Lift** ⬆️
- Acts **perpendicular to the relative wind** and upward
- Generated primarily by the **wings**
- Explained by Bernoulli's Principle (pressure differential) and Newton's Third Law (deflection of airflow downward)

## 2. **Weight** ⬇️
- Acts **downward** toward the center of the Earth (gravity)
- Opposes lift
- Includes the total weight of the aircraft, fuel, passengers, and cargo

## 3. **Thrust** ➡️
- Acts **forward**, parallel to the flight path
- Generated by the engine/propeller (or jet engine)
- Opposes drag

## 4. **Drag** ⬅️
- Acts **rearward**, opposing the aircraft's motion through the air
- Two main types:
  - **Parasite drag** — increases with speed (airframe, landing gear, etc.)
  - **Induced drag** — byproduct of lift generation, *decreases* with speed

---

## Opposing Force Pairs
| Force | Opposed By |
|-------|-----------|
| Lift | Weight |
| Thrust | Drag |

---

## Straight & Level Unaccelerated Flight
All four forces are **equal and balanced**:
- Lift = Weight
- Thrust = Drag

**Exam tip:** A common trap is assuming lift acts straight up — remember, lift is always *perpendicular to the relative wind*, which matters in turning flight! 🎯
